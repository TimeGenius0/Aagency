Most boards are asking "what's our AI spend?" when they should be asking something much harder.

I've sat in enough strategy sessions to know the questions that don't get asked are usually the ones that matter most. Here are 5 I almost never hear in the boardroom:

**1. What's our cost per decision, and is AI moving it?**
Not "are we using AI" — but is it actually making us faster and cheaper at the things that drive value?

**2. Where is AI compressing our moat instead of building it?**
If you're using the same tools as everyone else, you're not gaining advantage. You might be erasing it.

**3. What's the half-life of our current AI investments?**
Models improve every 6 months. The workflow you just built around GPT-4 might be obsolete before it's adopted.

**4. Are we measuring outputs or outcomes?**
"We deployed 3 AI tools" is an output. "We reduced churn by 8%" is an outcome. Most boards are tracking the wrong thing.

**5. What are we learning, and how fast?**
This is the one that separates leaders from laggards. ROI from AI isn't just financial — it's organizational learning velocity.

The companies that win the next 5 years won't necessarily be the ones who spent the most on AI. They'll be the ones whose boards asked better questions earlier.

Which of these is your board actually discussing — and which ones are still missing from the agenda?
