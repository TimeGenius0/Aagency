Most "AI transformation" is just AI theater.

I've watched organizations run pilot after pilot, host prompt engineering workshops, stand up AI task forces, and publish internal newsletters about "our AI journey" — all while their actual workflows remain completely unchanged.

Here are 6 signs you're performing transformation instead of executing it:

**1. Your AI initiatives live in PowerPoint longer than in production.**
Decks multiply. Deployments don't.

**2. You're measuring inputs, not outcomes.**
"We trained 400 employees on ChatGPT" is not a KPI.

**3. The people closest to the work aren't involved in the decisions.**
Leadership is excited. Practitioners are skeptical. Nobody's asking why.

**4. You have an AI policy but no AI experiments running.**
Governance before learning is fear dressed up as responsibility.

**5. Every use case requires a business case before anyone can try anything.**
You're optimizing for approval, not discovery.

**6. The word "transformation" appears in your strategy doc more than a specific workflow does.**
Vague language protects vague thinking.

The honest version of AI transformation is unglamorous. It's someone on your ops team automating a report they hated building. It's a support rep using a prompt they wrote themselves to cut handle time. It's small, it's messy, and it compounds.

The performed version looks great in board updates and does almost nothing.

The gap between the two isn't a technology problem. It's a leadership honesty problem.

Which one is your organization actually doing right now — and how do you know?
