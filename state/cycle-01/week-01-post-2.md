Most AI strategies fail before they start — because nobody actually knows what they're working with.

Before you decide what to build or buy, you need a brutally honest picture of where AI capability lives in your organization right now.

That's what a 30-day audit is for. And week one is deceptively simple: **just look.**

Here's how to run days 1–7:

**Map the work, not the tools.**
Don't start with "what AI software do we have?" Start with "what do our people actually spend time on?" Shadow three or four people across different functions for a day. You'll find repetitive decision-making, manual data wrangling, and copy-paste workflows everywhere. That's your raw material.

**Document the workarounds.**
Every spreadsheet duct-taped to a process is a signal. Every "we just manually check that" is a signal. These aren't inefficiencies — they're capability gaps with a cost attached.

**Ask one question in every meeting this week:**
*"If this task got 10x faster, what would change?"*
The answers will surprise you. Sometimes nothing changes. Sometimes everything does. That distinction matters enormously for build vs. buy.

You're not making decisions yet. You're building situational awareness.

The organizations that get AI right aren't the ones who moved fastest. They're the ones who actually understood their own operations before they started buying tools or spinning up dev teams.

Week one is about intellectual honesty before strategic ambition.
