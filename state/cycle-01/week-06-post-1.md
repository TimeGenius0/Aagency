We tried to build our own AI capability instead of buying off the shelf.

18 months later, here's what actually happened — not the board deck version.

**The honest breakdown:**

Month 1-3: Enthusiasm was high. The team was energized. We hired two ML engineers and told ourselves we were being "strategic."

Month 4-9: Reality hit. We spent more time on data infrastructure than actual AI. Classic. Nobody warned us that "building AI capability" is secretly 80% data cleanup and 20% everything else.

Month 10-14: We had something working. Barely. It was slower than the vendor tools we'd rejected, and our ML engineers were quietly updating their LinkedIn profiles.

Month 15-18: We made a call. Kept the internal capability for two very specific use cases where our proprietary data was genuinely the moat. Bought everything else.

**What we actually learned:**

- "Build" is almost never about the technology. It's about whether you have the data advantage to justify it.
- The vendor tools got dramatically better while we were building. That math doesn't favor you.
- Internal capability creates organizational muscle even when the tools lose. The team that struggled through the build became our best AI adopters.
- The 18 months wasn't wasted. But we could've gotten 70% of the outcome in 6.

The real mistake wasn't build vs. buy. It was not defining *why* we were building before we started.

Most companies make that same mistake with a much bigger budget.
